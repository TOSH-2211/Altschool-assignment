DELICIOUS JOLLOF RICE AND CHICKEN

Hearty Rice & Chicken Delight

JOLLOF RICE AND CHICKEN is a popular West African dish known for its rich, savory flavours and vibrant red-orange colour. The rice is cooked in a tomato-based sauce that includes ingredients like onions, bell peppers, garlic and a blend of spices such as thyme, curry powder and chili. It's often served with grilled, fried or roasted chicken that's marinated in spices for added flavour. Jollof rice is a staple in countries like Nigeria, Ghana and Senegal, with each having its own unique twist on the recipe. The dish is commonly enjoyed at parties, celebrations and family gatherings.

Recipe Information

  • Preparation Time: 20 minutes
  • Cook Time: 50 minutes
  • Servings: 6
  • Difficulty:Intermediate

INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups grain parboiled rice
  • 1 whole chicken cut into 6 big parts
  • 4 large tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 2 red bell pepper
  • 2 onion (sliced)
  • 2 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1/2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon thyme
  • 3 seasoning cubes
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Most importantly, 3 cups chicken stock (the water used in boiling the chicken)

STEP BY STEP INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Season the chicken with salt, curry, thyme and seasoning cubes. Cook until tender and reserve the stock.
  2. Blend tomatoes, bell pepper and one full onion until smooth.
  3. Heat oil in a pot, add sliced onions, then tomato paste. Fry for 2 minutes.
  4. Add blended tomato mixture and cook for about 10–15 minutes until reduced.
  5. Add chicken stock, check seasoning and pour in rinsed rice. Stir to combine.
  6. Reduce heat, cover with foil or lid and simmer until rice is cooked.
  7. Grill or fry the chicken and serve alongside the rice.

COOKING TIPS

For the best texture, always use parboiled rice, as it holds its shape beautifully and cooks evenly. Be sure to cover the pot tightly while cooking, this traps the steam and ensures the rice cooks perfectly fluffy and tender. To add a burst of colour and a boost of nutrition, consider stirring in vibrant vegetables like peas or carrots, making the dish not only more delicious but also visually appealing and wholesome.
